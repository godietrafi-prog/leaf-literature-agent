IMPORTANT CORRECTION: the version of this file written on the first pass (before full-text retrieval) contained numbers (59.52% purity, RuBisCO enrichment 48%->92%, "Nizo method") that do NOT appear anywhere in the actual paper - these were carried over from an unverified crashed-agent note and are now known to be wrong for this citekey (they may belong to a different, not-yet-identified source, or were fabricated/confused during the earlier session). Retracting those numbers.

Real findings: the authors explicitly tested THREE recovery routes and rejected two of them. Heat coagulation (70C, 1h) gave only 6% yield and 30% purity - rejected. Isoelectric point precipitation (pH 4.2) gave an insoluble protein that stayed insoluble even after raising pH back to 7 - rejected because low solubility blocks downstream functional use (foaming/gelling/emulsifying). The chosen route skips precipitation entirely: mild heat+pH coagulation (52C, pH6, 33min) removes chlorophyll/cell debris while leaving the RuBisCO-dominant fraction soluble, followed by pure membrane filtration (micro- -> ultra- -> dia-filtration), landing at 67.2% purity / 14.2% yield / 27% RuBisCO yield in the best (2020) batch, off-white color. This is a second strong existence-proof (alongside cloverbiorefinery2025) that a non-precipitating, membrane-based route can beat precipitation-based routes on functionality even at similar or lower purity numbers. Table 3 in the paper gives a valuable cross-species comparison: spinach (3% yield/95% purity, lab-scale ultra-pure), sugar beet leaf (11%/86.4%, "scalable"), duckweed (this paper, 14.2%/67.2%), siris (6%/37%), poplar (7.8%/60%) - confirming the purity-yield trade-off is general across leaf species, and that no source in this comparison table clears both this project's purity (>=50-60%) AND yield (>=30%) targets simultaneously using precipitation-based methods. Gelling data: at pH 7, duckweed protein gels nearly as strong as egg white and stronger than soy - a strong functional-property result independent of the purity number.
