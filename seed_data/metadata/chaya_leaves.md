Full text read (11 pages, pp.118-128). New citekey created per task instructions since chaya is not covered elsewhere in this review and the paper has genuine protein-extraction content (not just a composition baseline).

**Composition of raw chaya leaves (dry basis):** protein 32.09 +/- 0.48%, ash 7.88%, fiber 9.33%, fat 5.20%, carbohydrate 43.33%. This is a notably high leaf protein content, above most other species baselined in this review (e.g. carrot_composition_baseline's 18.71%).

**LPC extraction process:** alkaline solubilization at pH 13, filtration, acidification to pH 3.5 to precipitate protein, centrifugation (6000 rpm/4C/20 min), neutralization, and vacuum drying at 60C to below 15% moisture. Final LPC protein content (purity): **63.52 +/- 0.69%** - clears this project's 50-60% purity target band on its own. No extraction yield relative to starting leaf mass is reported anywhere in the paper - per this task's real-data-only rule, this is recorded as "not stated in this paper," not inferred or carried over from another species.

**RuBisCO confirmation:** SDS-PAGE shows bands at approximately 50 kDa and 10 kDa, interpreted by the authors as the RuBisCO large and small subunits respectively (consistent with, though slightly off from, the canonical ~55 kDa/~12.5-15 kDa values used elsewhere in this review - likely a gel-resolution/estimation difference, not a structurally different protein), and the paper states this pattern is comparable to alfalfa and spinach LPCs per cited literature. No quantitative % of total protein that is RuBisCO is given.

**Amino acid profile / nutritional quality:** Amino Acid Score (AAS) = 100% for ALL essential amino acids in both raw leaves and the final LPC against the FAO reference standard - i.e. no limiting amino acid in either the leaf or the extracted concentrate, an unusually clean nutritional result. Protein Efficiency Ratio (PER, three calculated variants): LPC PER1=1.71, PER2=2.54, PER3=1.28 (leaves: PER1=2.47, PER2=2.87, PER3=2.07 - i.e. the LPC scores somewhat lower than the raw leaf on PER1/PER3, an interesting caution that concentration/processing does not automatically improve protein quality scores). Essential-to-total amino acid ratio (E/T) for the LPC = 32.47%.

**Thermal stability:** DSC shows LPC protein denaturation temperature ~57C - notably LOWER than the 63-75C range cited elsewhere for other RuBisCO sources (cf. martin2014_spinach's 64.9C for spinach RuBisCO), suggesting chaya's RuBisCO (or its co-extracted protein mixture) may be somewhat less thermally stable than other species' - a potential caution for any downstream heat-processing step if chaya were used as a process-design analog.

**In vitro digestibility:** the LPC was found MORE bioavailable than a control diet at both the simulated stomach stage (1.35 vs 1.08 g/100mL) and small intestine stage (0.94 vs 0.78 g/100mL) - a positive functional/nutritional finding for the LPC specifically, independent of the purity/yield/color/flavor axes this review otherwise tracks.

**Polyphenol fate during processing (relevant to the color/off-flavor axis):** total polyphenol content of raw leaves was 3.0-3.5 mg GAE/g; the precipitation supernatant/remnant retained a similar level (~3.5 mg GAE/g), but the final PELLET (i.e., the LPC itself, after protein removal) dropped to 0.8 mg GAE/g. The authors interpret this as evidence that protein-polyphenol conjugates did NOT form during precipitation (i.e. polyphenols largely partition away from the precipitated protein fraction rather than binding to it) - a mechanistically interesting contrast to alfalfa_polyphenol_uf's finding (on a different species/process) that phenolics DO co-extract substantially with protein and require a dedicated resin-adsorption step to remove. Polyphenol identity via LC-MS/MS (Table 4): cyanidin, epicatechin, 3-chlorogenic acid, p-coumaric acid, vanillic acid, rutin - summarized as roughly 14.8% anthocyanidins, 61.0% hydroxycinnamic acids, 6.9% hydroxybenzoic acids, and 17.2% flavonols by class.

**No chlorophyll-removal % or off-flavor/sensory data reported anywhere in the paper** - recorded as "not stated in this paper" per the task's real-data-only instruction, not inferred from other species.
