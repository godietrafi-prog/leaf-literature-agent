Full text obtained (single-page abstract-level PDF, page1BCAB.pdf - appears to be an abstract/highlights capture rather than the complete article body, but contains the full quantitative results needed). CONFIRMS both existing numbers (66.93% purity / 10.22% yield, the initial un-optimized screening result) AND adds the previously-missing RSM-optimized figure the task flagged: at optimized conditions (extraction temperature 42.8C, amylase concentration 18,446 U, extraction time 4.44h), protein content reaches **89.41%** at a very similar yield of 9.56% (barely lower than the un-optimized 10.22%). This is a clean within-paper demonstration that RSM optimization of a single enzyme-assisted process can raise purity by ~22 percentage points (66.93% -> 89.41%) for almost no yield cost - one of the more favorable purity/yield trade-offs found anywhere in this review, and should be added to protocol_matrix.md's purity/yield frontier table as the optimized figure (89.41%/9.56%), not just the un-optimized screening figure.

Additional findings from the abstract: in vitro protein digestibility of the RLPC (radish leaf protein concentrate) was high (92.17%); protein fractionation showed glutelins as the dominant fraction, followed by prolamins, albumins, and globulins; apparent molecular weights of the RLPC and its isolated fractions ranged 35-92 kDa; threonine, methionine, and tryptophan were the most abundant amino acids; the RLPC showed considerable antioxidant activity (DPPH, FRAP, ABTS assays) and desirable functional properties (water/oil holding capacity, emulsion capacity/stability, low gelation concentration); microbial load remained in an acceptable range across 6 weeks of storage under both ambient and refrigerated conditions - i.e. this is a reasonably complete functional-characterization paper, not just a yield/purity optimization study, on the same species as radish_fh2025 but a different enzyme mechanism (starch-degrading alpha-amylase vs. cell-wall-degrading xylanase/cellulase in radish_fh2025).
