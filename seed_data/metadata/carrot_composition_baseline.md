Open access (PMC), full text obtained. Not an extraction protocol paper, but fills an important missing baseline: fresh carrot leaf composition is 18.71% crude protein, 15.69% fiber, 3.19% oil (dry basis), with total chlorophyll of 145.6 mg/100g and total carotenoids of 2588.9 mg/100g - carrot leaves are notably rich in ascorbic acid (186 mg/100g fresh - higher than spinach, celery, mint, parsley) and total phenolics (917 mg/100g dry basis). This is the first carrot-specific chlorophyll number found in this pass and should be used as the denominator when defining a quantitative "% chlorophyll removed" target for carrot-leaf experiments (analogous to nettle_foods2024's raw baseline of 4781.41 ug/g, which enabled that paper's precise 99.7% reduction claim). Drying/storage findings are tangential to extraction but relevant to a real pipeline: microwave-infrared drying best preserves overall bioactives, oven-drying at 40C best preserves vitamin C specifically, and refrigerated storage outperforms room-temperature storage for preserving phenolics/antioxidants/chlorophyll/carotenoids over 7 days - relevant if carrot tops need to be stored between harvest and processing.
