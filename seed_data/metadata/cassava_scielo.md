IMPORTANT CORRECTION: earlier metadata for this citekey (67% purity/6.2% yield for acid alone vs 76%/0.8% for heat+acid) does not match anything in the actual paper's 4-method comparison and has been retracted - likely another crashed-agent-era mixup, possibly with the ScienceDirect cassava_foodchem2023 paper or a third, not-yet-identified cassava source. Real paper (confirmed via duckweed_acs2021's own reference list, ref 18, Coldebella et al. 2013, Eng. Agric. 33:1223-1233) tests 4 distinct methods: temperature coagulation (61% purity, highest purity of the 4), isoelectric precipitation (~50-55% purity, fastest processing), alkaline solubilization + 100C drying (44% purity but highest yield at 59.7%, though brown-colored - a quality concern directly relevant to this project's color target), and natural fermentation (51.24% purity, 25.86% mass yield, lowest processing cost). Authors recommend IEP or fermentation for practical/industrial use, explicitly ruling OUT combining two extraction phases as not worth the added complexity. Useful as a same-species, same-paper 4-way comparison showing color (browning) as an explicit trade-off dimension alongside purity/yield, not just an afterthought.
