Found via targeted search specifically to check whether real-time electrochemical monitoring of leaf-wounding oxidative responses (the biological basis of the DGMA concept) has precedent in the plant science literature, independent of whether a "DGMA" or "blood gas analyzer analog" instrument by that name exists (it does not appear to - no hits found for that terminology in agriculture/plant contexts). These two papers confirm the underlying biology and instrumentation class are real and precedented: catalytic amperometry has been used to track superoxide anion radical (a reactive oxygen species, closely related to the LOX pathway's oxidative chemistry) generation in real time in wounded spinach leaves specifically - the same species class this project's literature review has repeatedly touched (spinach appears as a RuBisCO benchmark system throughout `martin2014_spinach` and others). Separately, in vivo imaging in wounded Arabidopsis showed that a lipoxygenase inhibitor completely suppresses singlet-oxygen formation - direct causal evidence that LOX is the source of the wounding-triggered oxidative signal, supporting the DGMA-based pO2/pCO2 kinetic logic described in ai_architecture_notes.md. Neither paper does food-processing off-flavor work or uses anything resembling a DGMA/BGA-analog instrument - this is basic plant physiology methodology, cited here as mechanistic/instrumental-plausibility support, not as a directly applicable protocol.
